Training Request Power App Showcase

A model-driven, request-workflow Power App designed to manage training requests through a structured four-stage lifecycle with role-based notifications and Dataverse integration.


Application Overview


The Training Request application streamlines submission, review, creation, and publication of training requests and their resulting training materials.. It is built using a model-driven Power App architecture leveraging Dataverse tables and automated workflows.

Data Model & Solution Objects


The solution consists of several objects that are breifly explained below:


  • TR_Headers (Primary Table) - Stores all training request records including the title, category, department, building, training document file uploads, and metadata.
  • TR_Roles - Contains user-assigned roles used to drive email notifications and approval routing logic. For example, Trainer and Trainer Manager roles receive notifications during the Training Review and Training Creation stages.
  • App - The model-driven Power App interface used by end users to create, review, manage, and track training requests.
  • Choices - There are 7 global choices which gives the user a drop-down menu selection or multi-selection options.
  • Cloud Flows - There are 2 Power Automate cloud flows that trigger on record creation and business process flow stage changes to send email notifications to users based on their assigned roles.
  • Connection References - The connection references link to both the Dataverse and Outlook 365 for the cloud flows to access data and send emails.
  • Environment Variables - There are two environment variables used to create the url link to the power app in the email notifications.
  • Processes - The business process flow guides the Training Request record through the four lifecycle stages.
  • Security Role - Access to the app is controlled through a custom security role that grants permissions to the TR_Headers and TR_Roles tables, as well as access to the app itself. Users must be assigned this role to interact with the application.
  • Site Map - Navigation structure that organizes tables, dashboards, and pages within the model-driven app.

Process Flow


The application follows a structured four-stage lifecycle:


  • Stage 1: Training Request
  • Stage 2: Training Review
  • Stage 3: Training Creation
  • Stage 4: Training Available

Stage Walkthrough

Email Notifications


It is importatnt to note that each stage of the Training Request process triggers email notifcations to users. All cloud flow email notification contain a hyperlink to the power app record for users to easily access the training request that they need to review or view. Below is a summary of the email notifications for each stage:


  • Record creation in the Training Request stage: an email is sent to the requestor notifying them that they created a new training request.
  • Training Review stage: an email is sent to the training team notifying them that a new training request is ready for review. If the request is rejected and the stage is pushed back to Training Request, then the original requestor is notified that their request was rejected.
  • Training Creation stage: an email is sent to the training team notifying them that the training request has been approved and is ready for training content creation.
  • Training Available stage: an email is sent to learners based on the department and/or building selections in the training request and the predefined roles in the TR_Roles table, notifying them that the training is now available.

Summary


This Power App demonstrates a full lifecycle workflow system integrating Dataverse, role-based notifications, and structured business process stages for training management.
